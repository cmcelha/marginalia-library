# Chapter 1 — Part I: Stratagems for advantageous situations (chapters 1-3)

## Unit 1 — Winning stratagems, stratagem 1: Deceive the heavens to cross the sea

> **1. Deceive the heavens to cross the sea** (瞞天過海, Mán tiān guò hǎi)
>
> The most familiar disguise is the most successful. Move in plain sight by dressing your real intent as the ordinary business of every day. Authorities and observers who see a routine repeated long enough cease to scrutinise it. Hide the unusual inside the usual.

## Unit 2 — Winning stratagems, stratagem 2: Besiege Wèi to rescue Zhào

> **2. Besiege Wèi to rescue Zhào** (圍魏救趙, Wéi Wèi jiù Zhào)
>
> When the enemy is too strong to be met head-on, attack what they cannot afford to lose. Force the strong to come to you, on ground of your choosing, by threatening something they must defend. The reference is to the Battle of Guiling (354 BCE), in which Sun Bin relieved the siege of Zhào by attacking Wèi's capital.

## Unit 3 — Winning stratagems, stratagem 3: Kill with a borrowed knife

> **3. Kill with a borrowed knife** (借刀殺人, Jiè dāo shā rén)
>
> When you cannot strike without cost, find someone who will strike for you. Direct an ally, a rival, or the enemy's own ambitions against the target. The damage is done; your hand is clean.

## Unit 4 — Winning stratagems, stratagem 4: Wait at ease for the labouring enemy

> **4. Wait at ease for the labouring enemy** (以逸待勞, Yǐ yì dài láo)
>
> Choose the time and the ground; let the enemy come to you tired. Provoke the enemy into long marches, futile pursuits, and divided attention. When they arrive exhausted, you arrive fresh. The advantage of position and rest decides the encounter before it begins.

## Unit 5 — Winning stratagems, stratagem 5: Loot a burning house

> **5. Loot a burning house** (趁火打劫, Chèn huǒ dǎ jié)
>
> When the enemy is consumed by internal trouble — famine, division, disease, corruption — strike. Do not let the moment of weakness pass; an enemy who recovers from internal trouble is harder to break than one who never had it. The cruel logic of the stratagem is that timing matters more than provocation.

## Unit 6 — Winning stratagems, stratagem 6: Clamour in the east, strike in the west

> **6. Clamour in the east, strike in the west** (聲東擊西, Shēng dōng jī xī)
>
> Direct the enemy's attention to one place and act in another. The feint must be loud enough to draw a real commitment of resources; only then is the silent flank truly undefended. Surprise is not the absence of warning — it is warning pointed in the wrong direction.

## Unit 7 — Enemy-dealing stratagems, stratagem 7: Create something from nothing

> **7. Create something from nothing** (無中生有, Wú zhōng shēng yǒu)
>
> Manufacture an apparent threat or asset where none exists; once the enemy commits to responding to the illusion, the real move follows. The stratagem works in two beats: a false thing that becomes credible through repetition, then a real thing slipped in behind the false. The enemy, accustomed to discounting your feints, fails to read the genuine one.

## Unit 8 — Enemy-dealing stratagems, stratagem 8: Openly repair the gallery road; secretly march to Chéncāng

> **8. Openly repair the gallery road; secretly march to Chéncāng** (明修棧道，暗渡陳倉, Míng xiū zhàn dào, àn dù Chéncāng)
>
> A more elaborate cousin of clamour-east-strike-west. Make a visible move that the enemy can watch and prepare for. While they are absorbed in defending against it, take a different road that the visible move concealed. The reference is to Han Xin's campaign against Sānqín in 206 BCE.

## Unit 9 — Enemy-dealing stratagems, stratagem 9: Watch the fires burning across the river

> **9. Watch the fires burning across the river** (隔岸觀火, Gé àn guān huǒ)
>
> When two enemies are fighting each other, do not interrupt them. Let them spend their strength on one another; intervene only when both are exhausted, and then decisively. Patience here costs less than any campaign.

## Unit 10 — Enemy-dealing stratagems, stratagem 10: Hide a knife behind a smile

> **10. Hide a knife behind a smile** (笑裏藏刀, Xiào lǐ cáng dāo)
>
> Win the enemy's trust through warmth, gifts, ceremony, and shared interest. The defences that resist a frontal assault fall to a friend. The stratagem is also a warning to recognise the smile of others when it does not match the deeds beneath.

## Unit 11 — Enemy-dealing stratagems, stratagem 11: Sacrifice the plum tree to save the peach

> **11. Sacrifice the plum tree to save the peach** (李代桃僵, Lǐ dài táo jiāng)
>
> Choose what to lose. When circumstances will not permit you to keep everything, give up the lesser to preserve the greater; designate a scapegoat or a decoy so that the central thing escapes. The hardest part is admitting that not everything can be kept.

## Unit 12 — Enemy-dealing stratagems, stratagem 12: Lead away the goat in passing

> **12. Lead away the goat in passing** (順手牽羊, Shùn shǒu qiān yáng)
>
> While pursuing the main plan, take whatever small opportunities present themselves along the way. Do not be so fixated on the grand stroke that you walk past the small wins. Many campaigns are decided by the accumulated weight of advantages no single one of which seemed worth the trouble.

## Unit 13 — Offensive stratagems, stratagem 13: Beat the grass to startle the snake

> **13. Beat the grass to startle the snake** (打草驚蛇, Dǎ cǎo jīng shé)
>
> Probe the unseen by provoking a reaction. A sudden, conspicuous act flushes hidden ambushes, exposes spies, or reveals an enemy's true plans. Read backwards, it is a warning: do not strike the grass yourself unless you mean to reveal what hides in it.

## Unit 14 — Offensive stratagems, stratagem 14: Borrow a corpse to return the soul

> **14. Borrow a corpse to return the soul** (借屍還魂, Jiè shī huán hún)
>
> Take a forgotten institution, an obsolete technology, a discarded slogan, or a fallen banner, and revive it for present use. What once belonged to others can be claimed by whoever first remembers it. The vehicle need not be respectable; it need only carry.

## Unit 15 — Offensive stratagems, stratagem 15: Lure the tiger off the mountain

> **15. Lure the tiger off the mountain** (調虎離山, Diào hǔ lí shān)
>
> Never attack a strong opponent in their stronghold. The position is half their strength; separate them from it. Once drawn into the open, the tiger fights with only half its power.

## Unit 16 — Offensive stratagems, stratagem 16: To catch, first let loose

> **16. To catch, first let loose** (欲擒故縱, Yù qín gù zòng)
>
> A cornered enemy fights to the death; an enemy who sees an opening fights to escape. Give them the opening — let the will to flee replace the will to fight — and the surrender will come more easily than the slaughter would have.

## Unit 17 — Offensive stratagems, stratagem 17: Toss a brick to draw a jade

> **17. Toss a brick to draw a jade** (拋磚引玉, Pāo zhuān yǐn yù)
>
> Offer something cheap to obtain something valuable in return. A small visible bait — a courtesy, a confession, a low first offer — draws out the larger response one was actually after. In conversation it is the modest opening that invites the better reply.

## Unit 18 — Offensive stratagems, stratagem 18: To catch bandits, first catch their leader

> **18. To catch bandits, first catch their leader** (擒賊擒王, Qín zéi qín wáng)
>
> If a force is held together by one person — by money, charisma, fear, or shared loyalty to a single commander — strike at that person and the rest collapses. Be careful with armies bound by vengeance or shared ideology: their leader's death may strengthen them.

# Chapter 2 — Part II: Stratagems for disadvantageous situations (chapters 4-6)

## Unit 19 — Mixed-warfare stratagems, stratagem 19: Pull the firewood from under the cauldron

> **19. Pull the firewood from under the cauldron** (釜底抽薪, Fǔ dǐ chōu xīn)
>
> Do not fight the boiling water; remove the fire beneath it. Attack the enemy's capacity to act — their supply, their morale, their financing, their alliances — rather than the army that capacity sustains. The indirect approach: deny the conditions that make the threat possible.

## Unit 20 — Mixed-warfare stratagems, stratagem 20: Muddy the water to catch the fish

> **20. Muddy the water to catch the fish** (渾水摸魚, Hún shuǐ mō yú)
>
> Create or exploit confusion so that distinctions blur and decisions become difficult. In the resulting chaos, take what you came for. Bureaucracies, markets, and battlefields are all vulnerable; in muddy water, the cautious lose first.

## Unit 21 — Mixed-warfare stratagems, stratagem 21: The cicada sheds its golden shell

> **21. The cicada sheds its golden shell** (金蟬脫殼, Jīn chán tuō ké)
>
> Leave behind the visible form — the camp, the banners, the customary appearance — so that what observers see is an empty husk while the substance has already departed. The stratagem of disengagement that buys time without inviting pursuit.

## Unit 22 — Mixed-warfare stratagems, stratagem 22: Shut the door to catch the thief

> **22. Shut the door to catch the thief** (關門捉賊, Guān mén zhuō zéi)
>
> Before delivering the final blow, close every avenue of escape. A wounded enemy who can run will recover and return; an enemy who cannot run must surrender or die. Patience in sealing the perimeter pays better than speed in striking.

## Unit 23 — Mixed-warfare stratagems, stratagem 23: Befriend the distant, attack the near

> **23. Befriend the distant, attack the near** (遠交近攻, Yuǎn jiāo jìn gōng)
>
> Conquer what you can hold; ally with what is too far to threaten. The neighbour you defeat becomes territory you can administer; the distant state you flatter cannot easily intervene. The doctrine that built the Qin empire.

## Unit 24 — Mixed-warfare stratagems, stratagem 24: Borrow the road to attack Guó

> **24. Borrow the road to attack Guó** (假途伐虢, Jiǎ tú fá Guó)
>
> Persuade an ally to lend you what is needed to defeat a common enemy. When the enemy is gone, turn on the ally with the resources they themselves provided. The story is from the Spring and Autumn period: Jin asked Yú for passage to attack Guó, and when Guó fell, Jin destroyed Yú on the way home.

## Unit 25 — Combined stratagems, stratagem 25: Replace the beams with rotten timbers

> **25. Replace the beams with rotten timbers** (偷梁換柱, Tōu liáng huàn zhù)
>
> Substitute weak supports for the structural members of the enemy's order — their key personnel, their procedures, their assumptions. When the load comes, what looked solid gives way. A stratagem of slow subversion rather than direct attack.

## Unit 26 — Combined stratagems, stratagem 26: Point at the mulberry to scold the locust

> **26. Point at the mulberry to scold the locust** (指桑罵槐, Zhǐ sāng mà huái)
>
> When direct confrontation with the powerful is not possible, criticise by analogy. Address the actual target by addressing a stand-in; the accusation registers, but the accused cannot answer without exposing their guilt.

## Unit 27 — Combined stratagems, stratagem 27: Feign foolishness, do not be mad

> **27. Feign foolishness, do not be mad** (假痴不癲, Jiǎ chī bù diān)
>
> Pretend incapacity. An opponent who underestimates you ceases to take precautions. The crucial second half of the title — *bù diān*, "but not mad" — is a warning to keep the inner judgement intact while the outer face plays the fool.

## Unit 28 — Combined stratagems, stratagem 28: Remove the ladder once they have climbed

> **28. Remove the ladder once they have climbed** (上屋抽梯, Shàng wū chōu tī)
>
> Lure the enemy into a position from which they cannot retreat, then take away the route by which they came. Forced to fight on impossible ground, they have only one direction left to move. A cruel stratagem; works in argument as well as war.

## Unit 29 — Combined stratagems, stratagem 29: Decorate the tree with false blossoms

> **29. Decorate the tree with false blossoms** (樹上開花, Shù shàng kāi huā)
>
> Make a small force look like a large one by borrowing the appearance of allies, terrain, banners, or visible activity. The enemy reacts to the apparent strength, not the real strength. Useful when you have less than you need and must seem to have more.

## Unit 30 — Combined stratagems, stratagem 30: Turn the guest into the host

> **30. Turn the guest into the host** (反客為主, Fǎn kè wéi zhǔ)
>
> Enter another's enterprise as a subordinate; over time, take it over from within. The host who invites a guest is often slow to notice when the guest begins to give orders to the staff. Used as both warning and instruction.

## Unit 31 — Defeat stratagems, stratagem 31: The beauty stratagem

> **31. The beauty stratagem** (美人計, Měi rén jì)
>
> When the enemy is too strong to be defeated by force, weaken them from within by introducing temptation. Beauty, flattery, and indulgence loosen the discipline of leaders, sow jealousy among ranks, and turn attention away from the work. The stratagem applies to whatever the enemy cannot resist, not only to people.

## Unit 32 — Defeat stratagems, stratagem 32: The empty-city stratagem

> **32. The empty-city stratagem** (空城計, Kōng chéng jì)
>
> When you have no defences and cannot fight, appear so unconcerned that the enemy concludes you must have hidden forces. The stratagem works once, against an enemy who knows your reputation for cunning. The famous instance is Zhuge Liang opening the gates of Xicheng and playing the qin on the wall.

## Unit 33 — Defeat stratagems, stratagem 33: The double-agent stratagem

> **33. The double-agent stratagem** (反間計, Fǎn jiàn jì)
>
> Sow discord among the enemy by feeding their spies false information, or by turning the enemy's spy into your own instrument. While they are absorbed in internal accusations and counter-accusations, their capacity to act outwardly collapses.

## Unit 34 — Defeat stratagems, stratagem 34: Inflict injury on the self

> **34. Inflict injury on the self** (苦肉計, Kǔ ròu jì)
>
> Stage a wound — real or apparent — that demonstrates one's own incapacity, or one's victimhood at the hands of a third party. The enemy lowers their guard before someone clearly broken; an apparent victim earns trust impossible to win otherwise. A stratagem that costs the user something real.

## Unit 35 — Defeat stratagems, stratagem 35: The chain of stratagems

> **35. The chain of stratagems** (連環計, Lián huán jì)
>
> No single plan should be relied on. In matters of weight, prepare several stratagems in series so that if one fails, the next is already in motion. The plans should also reinforce one another — each making the next more likely to succeed.

## Unit 36 — Defeat stratagems, stratagem 36: When all else fails, retreat

> **36. When all else fails, retreat** (走為上計, Zǒu wéi shàng jì)
>
> When the situation cannot be won, withdraw. Surrender is total defeat; compromise is half defeat; flight is not defeat. A force that escapes intact may fight again on better ground. The most famous of the thirty-six — the proverb that gave the collection its name.
